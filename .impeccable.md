# Design Context — Summer Trip Dashboard

## Users
- **Primary user**: Melissa — viewing a personal travel dashboard built for her by her partner
- **Context**: Checking trip details on phone or iPad, likely revisiting multiple times before and during the Jul 3–12 trip
- **Job to be done**: See what's planned, what's booked, what to expect each day — without needing to ask questions
- **Tech comfort**: Assume general consumer comfort, not power user — everything must be immediately obvious

## Brand Personality
**Warm, Excited, Polished**

- This is a love letter disguised as a dashboard — every detail should feel personally crafted, not auto-generated
- The excitement of a European summer trip should come through in the design: color, typography, micro-interactions
- Polished means no rough edges, consistent spacing, smooth transitions — but not sterile or corporate
- It should feel like flipping through a beautifully designed travel magazine, not booking a flight on Expedia

## Emotional Goals
- **Primary**: Excitement and anticipation — "I can't wait for this trip"
- **Secondary**: Confidence — "Everything is handled, I just need to show up"
- **Tertiary**: Delight — small touches that make her smile (passport stamps, sunset gradient, countdown)

## Aesthetic Direction

### Visual Tone
- **Costa Azul** — coastal European warmth: terracotta, ocean blue, olive green, golden hour light
- Editorial travel magazine quality — Playfair Display for headings gives it that *Condé Nast Traveler* feel
- Light and airy by default (day mode), warm and intimate at night (evening mode — café terrace, not tech-dark)

### Typography
- **Headings**: Playfair Display (400–800, italic available) — serif elegance
- **Body**: Outfit (300–700) — clean, modern, readable at small sizes
- **Scale**: 9px (labels) → 11px (tags) → 12px (body) → 13px (content) → 15px (card titles) → 22px (hero)

### Spacing & Radius
- Border radius: `14px` (panels), `12px` (cards), `10px` (timeline), `8px` (buttons/tabs)
- Card padding: `14px 16px` to `24px 28px`
- Grid gaps: `12px` (cards), `16px` (sections), `20px` (major)

### Easing
- Standard: `cubic-bezier(0.22, 1, 0.36, 1)` — smooth deceleration
- Hover: `0.2s` duration
- Transitions: `0.3s–0.6s` depending on scope

### Anti-references
- NOT a corporate travel booking site (no Expedia/Booking.com sterility)
- NOT a spreadsheet or Google Doc
- NOT a tech dashboard with neon-on-black
- NOT boring — if a section feels flat, it needs warmth, texture, or a micro-interaction

## Design Principles

1. **Personal over generic** — Every element should feel hand-crafted for this specific trip. Country flag emojis, destination-specific colors, named restaurants — not placeholder content.

2. **Mobile-first reading** — Melissa will primarily view on phone/iPad. Touch targets must be generous, text must be readable without zooming, grids must collapse gracefully.

3. **Excitement through detail** — The design should reward scrolling. Each section reveals something new — a photo spot, a restaurant, a weather forecast. Scroll reveals and staggered entries create the feeling of unwrapping the trip.

4. **Warm over cool** — When in doubt, lean warm. Burnt orange over steel blue. Playfair italic over Outfit bold. Sunset gradient over flat color.

5. **Polish is love** — Smooth transitions, consistent spacing, proper evening mode, accessible text contrast — these details say "I spent time on this for you."
